Good support starts with good tooling. I built the support and feedback systems that connect Supabase users to the team.

## The Support Form

The main support form needed to be both comprehensive and not overwhelming:

- **Category selection**: Questions, Problems, Feature Requests
- **Project selection**: Associate tickets with specific projects
- **Priority levels**: For paid tier users
- **File attachments**: Screenshots and logs
- **Severity indication**: Business impact assessment

## AI Assistant Integration

In 2025, I added an AI assistant callout to the support form:

Before submitting a ticket, users are prompted to try the AI assistant for immediate help with common questions. This:

- Reduces ticket volume for simple issues
- Provides instant answers
- Reserves human support for complex problems

## Feedback Widget

Beyond formal support, users need a quick way to share feedback:

```typescript title="Feedback categories"
const categories = [
  'Question',
  'Problem',
  'Feedback',
  'Feature Request',
  'Documentation',
]
```

The feedback widget appears throughout the dashboard, making it easy to report issues in context.

## Freshdesk Integration

Tickets route to Freshdesk for the support team:

- Questions and Problems → Support queue
- Feature Requests → Product backlog
- Documentation issues → Docs team

## Airtable for Structured Feedback

Product feedback goes to Airtable for analysis:

- Categorized by feature area
- Tagged with user tier
- Tracked over time for patterns
- Used for prioritization decisions

## Related Items

I added "Related Items" to the support form, automatically linking:

- Relevant documentation
- Status page incidents
- Known issues
- Community discussions

This helps users self-serve when possible.

## Evolution

The support system evolved with the product:

1. **Early**: Simple form to email
2. **Growth**: Freshdesk integration with routing
3. **Scale**: AI assistant + related items
4. **Mature**: Full ticket management with SLAs

## Impact

The support infrastructure handles thousands of tickets monthly while:

- Directing users to self-service options first
- Routing issues to the right teams
- Providing context for faster resolution
- Capturing feedback for product improvement

Good support tooling makes the team more effective and users happier.
